OM1

What is the reason for the OM?

Reason preview

Please select a reason to view preview.

The claimant seeks to challenge the decision of the Secretary of State that there was no good reason for their failure to attend their PIP assessment which resulted in their PIP being disallowed. The Secretary of State has now accepted that the claimant had good reason for their failure to attend their PIP assessment. As a consequence, applying the decision of the Upper Tribunal in OM v Secretary of State for Work and Pensions (PIP) (2017) UKUT 458 (AAC), the decision disallowing PIP has fallen away and the claimant's PIP claim can continue. The claimant has in effect been put back in the position he would have been in had the decision under challenge not been made. Pursuant to OM, this now means that the appeals has itself become nugatory and no further action is required either by the Secretary of State or the tribunal in order that the appeal be disposed of.

What is the reason for the OM?

The claimant seeks to challenge the decision of the Secretary of State that there was no good reason for their failure to attend their PIP assessment which resulted in their DLA terminating and PIP being disallowed. The Secretary of State has now accepted that the claimant had good reason for their failure to attend their PIP assessment. As a consequence, applying the decision of the Upper Tribunal in OM v Secretary of State for Work and pensions (PIP) (2017) UKUT 458 (AAC), it is accepted (I) that the existence award of DLA has not terminated it has been re-instated and arrears paid; and (ii) the conversion process to PIP can continue - the claimant has in effect been put back in the position he would have been in had the decision under challenge not been made. Pursuant to OM, this now means that the appeal has itself become nugatory and no further action is required either by the Secretary of State or the tribunal in order that the appeal is disposed of.
